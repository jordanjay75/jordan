I spent my undergraduate years at Cornell fiddling away with government and infomration science. Connecting these two domiains of seemingly discongruent work was my intiruge into understanding how humans behaved when immersed and/or intermediated by technology — moslty robotic or social media-ey in nature.

I'm currently pursuing a masters in Human-Computer Interaction at Carnegie Mellon where I'm fascinated in understnading nuances within human-computer collaboration. Why? because developing systems by which we facilitate such collaboration has become increasingly prickly as our understanding of what separates the human from the machine — the mechanical infrastructure of orchestrated signals and wires from that of our own brain — has blurred and abstracted as technology develped. With the ubiquity of the Internet, these systems of information processing and cross-channel communication will ultimately link the psychological, verbal, textual, and binary models of interaction between objects and information.

I'm not a designer, but am committed to the exploration, development, and commercialization of products that support human-centric design processes and inclusivity.
