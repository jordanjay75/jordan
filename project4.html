Human Dynamics Lab (an HRI study)

Affective Grounding: Robo Humor as an H2H Intermediary

Our team looked at the afftects of robot delivered humor as an intermediary between two humans who were conversing over contentious topics. The purpose was to analyze how humans responded when intrrupted by an intelligent assistant: were they willing to ground their emotions and work effectively, or was there any affect at all?

Robots in context of human pairs have been studied extensively in industry. The main concentration in on seeing how weel humans and robots can work together in group collaborative setting - that is setting of 3 or more individuals. By uncovering some of the nuances in HRI, researchers and our team can get a step closer to seeing full human rovbot parity.

This project was conducted as a joint effort between Dr. Susan Fussell's Human Dynamics lab at Cornell University. This study on examining risk aversion in HRI collaborative work was a result of further exploration of this research.
