Data-Driven Display

A deep dive into data sense-making and visualization

The Atlantis Transportation Hub marks the epicenter of navigation to and around the San Juan Islands — a small archipelago just north of Seattle.

With varying modes of intra-island tranport, the Municipality of the Greater San Juan Islands, who transporatation division is headed by Elizabeth Duarte, sought a visual means to educate Atlantis customers on the ease, value, and flexibilty of trnasportation options. Commisioned to see this through, our team did just that.

Tasked with sifting through a static data set with the goal of exploring, structuring, then modeling the data to create meaning, we were presented with a sea of travel logistics from Atlantis hub around and around San Juan.

This unstructured data presented challenges as we began unpacking meaning behind its wall of numbers — mapping out stakeholder expectations, and identifying travel patterns that could then be roped into a succinct visual design.

Through our initial data dives, construction of stakeholder personas, and several visualizations of potential interfaces addresssing those personas, we were able to navigate the data whilst continuously pruning our grasp on the information that was given to us.

Extracting latent data structures and uncovering ways to organize information led us to constantly reengage with stakeholder wishes, embrace ambiguity until we found a smidgen of order, and at the end, design a product that directly addressed our user goals.
